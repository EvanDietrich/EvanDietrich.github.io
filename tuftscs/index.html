Tufts

Overview

I served as a TA in the Computer Science Department across all 4 of my years at Tufts, allowing me to meet, instruct, and learn from hundreds of students in our undergrad and grad programs. It is important to me that all students find CS welcoming and accessible, regardless of individual background or prior computing experience.

Background

Looking to contribute to the department, I joined the Teaching Staff for Prof. Strange's Discrete Mathematics course. Here, I led office hours and review sessions for students studying proof techniques, induction, modular arithmetic and cryptography, graphs, connectedness and more.

While greatly enjoying the theory and proofs of Discrete, I decided to test my abilities by instructing code-based material in our Intro to CS course, taught in C++ and directed by Prof. Monroe and Prof. Sheldon. Here, I led labs and grading meetings as students tackled recursion, OOP, and data structures.

After several semesters with Intro to CS, I sought a new challenge and TA'd a grad-level course, Computational Models in Cognitive Science. Here I led office hours, grading, and wrote both curriculum + assignments. Topics ranged from Connectionist and Bayesian models to agent-based simulation. Python, R, and JAGS (for simulations using Markov chain Monte Carlo) were employed throughout.
