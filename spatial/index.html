Undergraduate Research

Human-Robot Interaction

Eager to test out CS in Academia, I joined Prof. Scheutz's HRI Lab in early 2018, assisting a Doctoral student in extending the capabilities of a network modeling language-switching costs.

Working in Python, I wrote novel lexical methods and restructured the internal codebase of our interactive activation model, using bilingual lexicon data to explain the rarity of codeswitching mistakes in bilingual speech production.

Spatial Cognition

After getting a sense of CS research and concluding my project at the HRI Lab, I joined Tufts' Spatial Cognition Lab in May 2018. Here, I assisted in research on the connection between individual differences in spatial thinking and STEM learning, using those connections to develop assessments and interventions.

I self-taught R and STATA to execute statistical analysis, conducted robust literature reviews, and wrote extensive manuscript.
