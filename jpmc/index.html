Summer 2020 Overview

In 2020, intern teams were paired with JP Morgan nonprofit partners affected by Covid-19. Our product, a mobile-first web application, connects our nonprofit's employees with their small-business clients by means of appointment scheduling, messaging, and financial health evaluation.

I took charge of building our platform around a data analytics engine: a clustering model, categorizing the nonprofit’s clients based on their need, site engagement, and financial records. This allows us to provide attention and financial support proportional to small-business clients’ needs.

Product Functionality

  • Our front-end, a mobile-first GUI, begins with registration & login pages. Upon entrance, users are prompted to provide additional small-business-specific data forms before accessing the private messaging interface with our nonprofit's employees.
  • The back-end contains full, secure, database integration between user's profiles, and our data analytics engine.
  • Our analytics engine calculates a need and engagement index for each user/small-business representative, allowing for feed personalization based on level of need and financial concern. Different user clusters will recieve unique flagging for nonprofit employee's awareness, as well as automated drip chain messaging, ensuring that adequate assistance/contact is proportionally provided by our nonprofit partner to each of their clients.

Tech Stack

  • The front-end consists of Django and Bootstrap 4.
  • Back-end: Django and SQLite.
  • Data analytics was completed with Python, and the Matplotlib and SendGrid libraries.
