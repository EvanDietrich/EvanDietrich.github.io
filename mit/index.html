CIRCLE: Calibration of Infrared Cameras for Lasercom Evaluation

description

Our team was tasked with completing a full life-cycle project: Designing and developing an automated system that would run calibration tests on laser-communication terminals, capable of sending and receiving data across any atmospheric medium at light-speed.

Our team took the runtime of a calibration test series from 2-3 weeks, down to overnight completion.

ENVOY: Enabling Natural-language Versatility & Opportunity

Description

A comprehensive system to improve the machine translation of ASL with a design informed by Signing communities.

Current approaches to the machine translation of American Sign Language (ASL) neglect essential language nuances, data-processing techniques, and make decisions based on the preoccupations of the hearing community. Over the last two years, I have focused my team’s efforts to better-distinguish ASL signs that vary slightly in presentation but hold different meanings. A computationally-expensive process, we train models and leverage the capabilities of the Satori supercomputing cluster. ASL translation is an intellectually demanding endeavor, as several individually-difficult areas must work in harmony. We implement computer vision to run our system, capturing users’ movements and facial features in real-time. This year, I worked on feature extraction and natural language processing for language modeling, as the conversion of ASL to English is not a one-to-one translation.

We faced struggles early on due to the limitations of pre-existing databases containing studio-quality translation videos, which neglected variations in lighting, background, and signer skin tone. To ensure an equitable design, I am employing novel data mining and augmentation techniques; finding automated methods to generate labelled datasets from CSPAN footage and other public sources, while expanding the diversity of signers. I further fortify our datasets by training our convolutional network models against fake data generated from adversarial networks.
