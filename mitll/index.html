MIT Lincoln Laboratory

Overview

As part of MIT Lincoln Laboratory's Summer Research Program, I worked on a team of graduate students in completing a full life-cycle project: Designing and developing an automated system that would run calibration tests on laser-communication terminals, capable of sending and receiving data across any atmospheric medium at light-speed.

Our team took the runtime of a calibration test series from 2-3 weeks, down to overnight completion.

Technical Summary


Project motivation, software, threading architecture, image processing, hardware schematic, and user interface summaries are delineated in our technical poster. The PDF version of which can be found here.
