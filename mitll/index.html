MIT Lincoln Laboratory

Overview

A system that automates the calibration of infrared cameras, central to data transmission via laser beam. Our team took the runtime of a calibration test series from 2-3 weeks, down to overnight completion.

Background

I joined Lincoln as a Summer Research Program Intern, where I worked on a team of graduate students to complete a full life-cycle project.

Our task: to design and develop an automated system that would run calibration tests on laser-communication terminals, capable of sending and receiving data across any atmospheric medium at light-speed.

CIRCLE: Calibration of Infrared Cameras for Lasercom Evaluation

Description

Project motivation, software, threading architecture, image processing, hardware schematic, and user interface are delineated within our technical poster. The PDF version of which can be found here.
