Hi, my name is Evan

I'm currently finishing up my senior year at Tufts University, where I am TA'ing the grad-level course: Computational Models in Cognitive Science. Previously, I have worked as an Undergrad Technical Researcher and Intern at MIT Lincoln Laboratory, and as a Software Engineer Intern at J.P. Morgan.

My interests reside in machine learning, algorithm development, statistics, and neuroscience. Recent projects include improving the machine translation of American Sign Language via facial features (MIT-LL; 2019-20), and designing unsupervised learning models to classify nonprofit clients based on their need, engagement, and financial records (JPM; 2020).

I am actively considering full-time + intern positions with start dates in 2021. Anticipated to graduate in May 2021, I will complete my undergraduate career with a B.S. in Computer Science, a B.S. in Cognitive & Brain Sciences, and a minor in Econ from Tufts University.

Select Projects
  1. Logistic Regression for Cancer-Risk Screening
    Classifier (built on features such as age, family history, chemical markers) that determines if a patient is at risk of cancer and if a biopsy operation should take place. Maximized a true positive rate (TPR) while achieiving minimum 98% positive predictive value (PPV)
  2. Image Compression & Decompression
    A program that takes portable pixmap format (PPM) images and compresses them by transforming color spaces and discarding information not easily seen by the human eye
  3. Android Malware Analysis
    Reverse engineering an Android app to determine location and purpose of live malware
  4. Reinforcement Learning in a 2-D GridWorld
    Used SARSA and Q-Learning algorithms to determine optimal policy for an AI agent
  5. Heart Failure Prediction
    95.0% predictive accuracy with optimized model. Feature selection and classifier comparison across clinical dataset.
  6. Analysis of AMD64 Assembly Code
    Analyzed and debugged C procedures given in executable binary to prevent explosion of a "binary bomb" during runtime
  7. An Incident Alarm

  8. Image Classification: Sneakers vs Sandals
    Explored feature transformations to improve predictive accuracy
  9. Image Classification: Handwritten Digits

  10. Sentiment Analysis: Amazon/IMDB/Yelp Reviews
    Developed binary classifiers to generate sentiment-labels from new sentences
  11. A Universal Machine

  12. Simlines
