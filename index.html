Code For Good

>> In October 2019, I travelled to Columbus, Ohio to take part in J.P. Morgan's annual 'Code for Good' Hackathon. Upon arrival, I met the 4 other CS undergrads placed into my team, as well as the representatives from our designated nonprofit client, Apprisen.

>> After introductions and the release of our challenge, I took charge of our team to build and launch a Flask web + mobile application that met Apprisen's needs: Bolstering user engagement and increasing the likelihood of their users graduating from unique Debt Management Programs. Noticing the opportunity to provide value to our client via the mass of currently unused data from past users, I assembled regression models that determine the likelihood of new users to follow through with and graduate from their program, so that users deemed to be 'at risk' could be given more attention by Apprisen team members.

>> I broke down our project into sprints and delegated tasks to my team members for our 4pm-10am build period. I also lead the build of our UI and did some routing via Flask. After the build, all 14 teams attending presented to our client and J.P. Morgan tech leads in rotations. After this grading, our team was one of four selected for the final presentation stage. After 30 hours awake and final presentations, we were awarded second place for our client, and then went off to take a nap.

ENVOY: Enabling Natural-language Versatility and Opportunity

>> ENVOY is a system in development to help American Sign Language (ASL) users and English speakers communicate in real time - either in person or through video conferencing. The vast majority of recent attempts at ASL translation neglect to foster two-way communication and miss the importance of facial expression in ASL. These attempts also make decisions based on the preoccupations of the hearing community, instead of being designed in a more culturally appropriate and user-centric manner.
