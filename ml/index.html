Machine Learning and AI

Overview

Machine learning is one of the most significant disruptors in human history, greatly surpassing the predictive accuracy of old-style models. This capacity has compelled me to know how computers – typically beholden to static, deterministic processes – may ostensibly understand dynamic situations and devise solutions on their own.

Throughout undergrad, I’ve extended my intuition of this domain – determining appropriate modeling approaches for seemingly chaotic environments, and producing high-impact programs for leading industries (finance, healthcare, technology).

  • Multiple Linear Regression for Market Analytics
    Perhaps the most-used technique in modern quantitative finance.
  • Naive Bayesian Classifier for Radar
    Categorizing flying objects captured from noisy radar track data.
  • Logistic Regression for Cancer-Risk Screening
    Determines patient cancer risk, and if biopsy operation should take place. Maximized a true positive rate (TPR) while achieiving minimum 98% positive predictive value (PPV).
  • Reinforcement Learning in a 2-D GridWorld
    Used SARSA and Q-Learning algorithms to determine optimal policy for an AI agent.
  • Classifier Comparison for Heart Failure Prediction
    95% predictive accuracy with optimized model. Feature selection and classifier comparison across clinical dataset.
  • Image Classification: Sneakers vs Sandals
    Explored feature transformations to improve predictive accuracy.
  • Image Classification: Handwritten Digits
    Examined inverse penalty strength to classify image-as-CSV pixel data.
  • Sentiment Analysis: Amazon/IMDB/Yelp Reviews
    Developed gradient classifiers to generate sentiment-labels for novel sentences.
  • Sentiment Analysis: Twitter Data and Market Shifts
    Analyzing tone and sentiment of prominent political and industry figures as they correlate with market movements, based on company and industry-specific tweet content.
  • CIFAR-10 Object Recognition
    Experiments with convolutional neural networks, batch normalization, pooling.
  • Sudoku Solver with Constraint Satisfaction
    Implemented with backtracking & backjumping AI algorithms.
  • Autonomous Robot Model
    Behavior tree model operating under simple reflex rules.
  • Genetic Algorithm for the Backpack Problem
    Determines optimal item distribution from value-based storage constraint.
  • Neural Network for Plant Classification
    Back-propagation on multi-layered network, from scratch.
  • Interactive Activation Model for Bilingual Language Comprehension
    Using bilingual lexicon data to explain the rarity of codeswitching mistakes in bilingual speech production, incorporating inhibitory control.
  • Computer Vision for Sign Language Recognition
    Recognizing the manual features, facial features, and movements of signers in real-time.
  • Machine Translation of American Sign Language
    Extensive research involving CV, NLP, DL, and more.
  • Clustering Model for Nonprofit
    Classifies a nonprofit’s clients based on their need, site engagement, and financial records. Allows the nonprofit to provide attention and financial support proportional to their small-business clients’ needs.
  • Regression Model for Loan Repayment
    Predicts loan repayment likelihood from previously unused client data.

While building software, I’ve picked up the ability to use a mix of helpful third-party APIs, database technologies and query languages, scripting languages, version control systems, and build tools to aid in the process.
